About Me

I am a human rights barrister employed by Bryce Lyall Barrister. I have over 10 years of experience working primarily in refugee, immigration and Tiriti o Waitangi-related law.

I help tangata whenua and migrants navigate difficult legal challenges with practical advice and effective advocacy. My approach is solutions-focused and I work toward outcomes that consider the wider realities my clients are facing.

I'm known for being a fearless advocate who prepares thoroughly and speaks up when it counts. Above all, I deeply care about the people I represent.

Why I Do This Work

Because everyone deserves to be treated with dignity.

Because the law should be used to protect, not harm.

Because advocacy, when done with care, can change lives.

Practice Areas

MACA Act 2011

Marine and Coastal Area (Takutai Moana) Act services for recognition of Customary Marine Title (CMT) and Protected Customary Rights (PCR) in the coastal marine area.

Funding available through Te Tari Whakatau.

Waitangi Tribunal

Waitangi Tribunal services include advancing claims related to all contemporary Crown breaches of Te Tiriti o Waitangi.

Funding available through Legal Aid.

Immigration

Immigration services include applications for all relationship-based visas, s 61 and special direction requests, and representation before the Immigration and Protection Tribunal for humanitarian, deportation and residence appeals.

Refugee

Refugee law services include representation in refugee and protection claims before the Refugee Status Unit and appeals to the Immigration and Protection Tribunal.

Funding available through Legal Aid.
